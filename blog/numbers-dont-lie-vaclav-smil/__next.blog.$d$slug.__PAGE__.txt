2:Tb93,<p>Vaclav Smil's <em>Numbers Don't Lie</em> is a collection of 71 short essays, each built on a single quantitative insight. The premise is simple: numbers are more reliable than narratives, but only if you understand what they mean in context.</p>
<h2 id="people">People</h2>
<ul>
<li>Infant mortality is a more discerning indicator of quality of life than income averages or the Human Development Index.</li>
<li>The easiest way to improve a child's chances of growing taller is for them to drink more milk.</li>
<li>Happiness: if you can't fit into the top 10 (Nordic, Dutch, Swiss, Kiwi, or Canadian), convert to Catholicism and start learning Spanish.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="countries">Countries</h2>
<ul>
<li>EU vs USA: 27 of the top 30 countries in quality of life are European.</li>
<li>UK: Once the unrivaled inventor of modern manufacturing — now deindustrialized. Per capita GDP is now just over half of the Irish mean.</li>
<li>China's 1978 economic modernization mirrors Japan's trajectory in the 1980s. Air/water pollution, aging population, limited gas resources.</li>
<li>Mean duration of empires: 220 years. Longest: Mesopotamian Elam at 10 centuries.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="machines-and-design">Machines and design</h2>
<ul>
<li>The 1880s were the decade that shaped modernity: electricity, internal combustion engines, lightbulbs, transformers, power plants.</li>
<li>Moore's law held for decades, but innovations are now slower.</li>
<li>There are fundamental differences between accumulated data, useful information, and insightful knowledge.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="food">Food</h2>
<ul>
<li>Nitrogen is the most important macronutrient for crops. Synthetic ammonia (solid urea) is what feeds the world.</li>
<li>In the US, food enough for ~230 million people was wasted in 2020 alone.</li>
<li>Japan's longevity secret: remarkably moderate food consumption — 25% less than US/France. The Chinese precept: <em>hara hachi bun me</em> — belly eight parts full.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="environment">Environment</h2>
<ul>
<li>Cattle zoo-mass is now more than 50% larger than human biomass.</li>
<li>Mobile phone production (annual) consumes as much energy as New Zealand uses in a year.</li>
<li>The quest for untested technical fixes is the curse of energy policy. Why not start with what is proven? Triple-glazed windows cost 15% more than double panes but have dramatically longer payback.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="the-throughline">The throughline</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>Numbers may not lie, but individual perceptions of them differ.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Smil's work is a masterclass in calibrating intuition with data. Recommended for anyone who's ever argued about the state of the world at dinner.</p>
